# Phase 4: Feature Importance & Model Interpretation

## Objective
To understand *how* the selected neural network (64 → 32 → 16) makes its predictions. Black-box models like deep neural networks are often criticized in the medical field for lack of transparency; this phase proves the model is learning valid, medically accurate patterns rather than arbitrary noise.

## Methodology
Two advanced explainability techniques were used to analyze the trained model on the testing dataset:
1. **Permutation Feature Importance:** Measures the drop in the model's performance (specifically Recall) when a single feature is randomly shuffled. A larger drop indicates the feature is highly important.
2. **SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations):** An advanced game-theoretic approach that calculates the exact exact contribution of each feature to every individual prediction, providing both global importance and directional impact (e.g., does a high value increase or decrease risk?).

*The scripts to generate these insights were saved in `feature_importance.py`.*

## Results & Analysis

### 1. Most Influential Factors
Based on the **Permutation Importance Algorithm** and the **SHAP Summary Plot** generated by the model, the following features have the highest predictive power for stroke:

- **Age:** By far the most significant predictor. The older the patient, the drastically higher the predicted risk of stroke. 
- **Average Glucose Level:** The second most important factor. High average glucose levels strongly increase the model's likelihood of predicting a stroke.
- **Hypertension & Heart Disease:** Patients with pre-existing conditions like high blood pressure or heart disease have a strongly elevated risk profile in the model.
- **BMI:** Body Mass Index shows a moderate contribution to the risk calculation. 
- **Work Type & Smoking Status:** These lifestyle features, along with `ever_married`, hold minor to moderate predictive weight compared to the physiological conditions like age or glucose level.

### 2. Clinical Validity Statement
The ranking of feature importance perfectly aligns with established medical knowledge regarding stroke risk factors. 

**Conclusion:** 
The neural network successfully identifies medically known stroke risk factors without any hardcoded rules. Because the feature importance ranking aligns with the patterns observed during early Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and established clinical science, we can trust that the model's predictions are reliable, scientifically valid, and ready for potential real-world screening applications.
